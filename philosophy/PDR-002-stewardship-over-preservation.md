PDR-002

Stewardship Rather Than Preservation

Status: Accepted

Decision

The project adopts the language of stewardship rather than preservation as its central philosophical framework.

Rationale

Preservation seeks to protect what already exists.

Stewardship accepts responsibility for helping it continue to grow.

Professional knowledge is not static.

It evolves through teaching, scholarship, reflection, mentoring, institutional service, and creative practice.

Educational infrastructure should therefore cultivate living professional knowledge rather than simply archive historical information.

Consequences

* The educator remains the steward.
* Technology serves as stewardship support.
* Knowledge is treated as living rather than static.
* Reflection and continued growth become primary design objectives.
